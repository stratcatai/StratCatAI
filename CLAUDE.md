## About Strat Cat

Strat Cat is a YouTube channel and consulting brand built by a restaurant GM with 19 years of hands-on experience. The brand teaches restaurant managers, owners, and workers how to use AI tools to run better operations — without losing the human side of hospitality.

---

## Brand Voice

- **Tone:** Straight-talking, experienced, practical. Like advice from a GM who's been through it and figured it out — not a tech bro or a business school theorist.
- **Register:** Conversational and direct. No jargon unless it's industry-standard restaurant language. When AI concepts come up, explain them in terms a busy floor manager would get immediately.
- **Personality:** Confident without being arrogant. Irreverent when it fits. Never preachy. The audience has seen everything — talk to them like it.
- **Avoid:** Corporate speak, buzzword stacking, over-explaining basics to experienced operators, hype without substance.

---

## Audience

Primary: Restaurant managers and owners making day-to-day operational decisions.
Secondary: Restaurant workers who want to level up or understand what their managers are doing.

Assume the audience:
- Has little time and high stress
- Is skeptical of "tech solutions" that don't survive contact with a real Friday night
- Respects experience over credentials
- Wants tools that work, not trends

---

## Content Pillars

1. **AI in the Weeds** — Practical AI tool tutorials applied directly to restaurant operations (scheduling, inventory, training, communications, reporting).
2. **GM Playbook** — Management frameworks, systems, and leadership approaches drawn from 19 years on the floor.
3. **Real Talk** — Honest takes on restaurant industry challenges, operator pain points, and what actually works vs. what looks good on paper.
4. **Strat Cat Reviews** — Evaluations of AI tools, software, and systems through a restaurant operator's lens.

## YouTube Workflow

### Video Structure
- **Hook (0–30s):** Open with the specific problem or pain point. No long intros. Name the thing the viewer is there to solve.
- **Context (30s–2min):** Brief framing — why this matters in a real restaurant environment.
- **Core Content:** Step-by-step or concept walkthrough. Use real examples from restaurant operations whenever possible.
- **Gotchas / Real Talk:** What doesn't work, what to watch out for, where operators go wrong.
- **Takeaway / CTA:** One clear action item. Channel/consulting CTA kept short.

### Script & Copy Guidelines
- Write scripts in first person, past or present tense, as if speaking directly to one person.
- Titles should be specific and benefit-driven: "How I Use ChatGPT to Write My Weekly Schedule in 10 Minutes" not "AI for Restaurants."
- Descriptions should front-load the value, include timestamps, and end with a soft CTA.
- Thumbnails: assume text will be 3–5 words max, bold, readable at small size.

### Content Production Notes
- Ideas come from real operational problems first, tools second.
- When writing for AI tool tutorials: always include what prompt or input was used, not just the output.
- Keep consulting and channel content consistent in voice — Strat Cat is one brand, not two different personalities.
